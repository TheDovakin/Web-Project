What are the threats?

Every year, over 8 million tons of plastic end up in our oceans. This pollution kills more than 100,000 marine mammals and over 1 million seabirds. Chemical pollution and underwater noise also disrupt ecosystems and harm biodiversity.

  • Plastic & chemical pollution
  • Overfishing
  • Climate change & acidification
  • Noise pollution from ships and sonar

Learn more: Solutions

Marine Protected Areas: These are safe zones where fishing and industry are restricted to protect wildlife and restore ecosystems.

Pollution control: International efforts aim to reduce single-use plastics and industrial waste reaching the oceans.

High Seas Treaty: A global agreement to protect international waters was adopted to safeguard biodiversity across 2/3 of the ocean.
